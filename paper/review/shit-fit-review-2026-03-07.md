# SHIT Editor Review / SHIT 主编审稿意见

**Date / 日期**  
2026-03-07

## Findings First / 先说 findings

### Finding 1: the abstract still tries to sell too many things at once

The current abstract is informative, but it behaves more like a proposal summary than a SHIT-friendly editorial hook. It names too many layers before it establishes the one line a reader should immediately remember.

**Editorial direction / 修改方向**  
Lead with the one painful truth: in an advisor group chat, a reply is not just a reply but a timed performance of reliability. Then foreground only four headline findings:

- inverse-U reply window
- call-out effect
- first-responder discount
- advisor red-envelope effect

Keep publication U-shape and folk species as second-order enrichments, not abstract-level overload.

### Finding 2: the results are good, but some sections still end too politely

The manuscript is strongest whenever the result section ends with a sentence that sounds impossible and then immediately feels true. Right now several result paragraphs still stop at competent explanation instead of landing the SHIT-style aftertaste.

**Editorial direction / 修改方向**  
Each result section should end with one deadpan sentence that could almost be screenshot on its own.

### Finding 3: the folk taxonomy is vivid but not yet institutional enough

`gatekeeping dog`, `muddy-water fish`, and `dragging bottle` are memorable. But they become much stronger when the paper treats them as stable offices in the laboratory constitution rather than as passing nicknames.

**Editorial direction / 修改方向**  
For each role, clarify:

- what the role does
- why the role emerges
- what it does to the reply window

This keeps the humor precise instead of decorative.

### Finding 4: the U-shaped relation between publication stock and reply enthusiasm needs a cleaner mechanism

The claim is strong, but it still risks reading like an inspired observation unless the paper states the mechanism more bluntly: the least published reply early to prove value, the most entangled reply early because the task is already theirs, and the middle delays because it can hide.

**Editorial direction / 修改方向**  
State the mechanism in one clean sentence and add one compact numerical contrast in the results.

### Finding 5: the manuscript should keep borrowing technique from adjacent parody journals, but not their identity

Compared with [Rubbish](https://rubbish-journal.org/zh/about), [The Journal of Immaterial Science](https://www.immaterialscience.org/), [JABDE](https://jabde.com/about/), [Annals of Improbable Research](https://improbable.com/whatis/), and [SpecGram](https://specgram.com/about.html), the paper is strongest when it absorbs their deadpan editorial technique without losing SHIT's small-scene pain.

**Editorial direction / 修改方向**  
Borrow:

- formal seriousness
- table/caption humor
- sustained pseudo-scholarly consistency

Do not borrow:

- sprawling world-building
- concept inflation
- humor that depends on niche English-only parody traditions

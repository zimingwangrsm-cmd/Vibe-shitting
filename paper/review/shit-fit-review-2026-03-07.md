## Findings First / 先说结论

### Finding 1: the older draft spine has more flavor

The manuscript reads better when it stays with the original `Read, Seen, Ignored / 已读、不回、稍后回复` frame and a handful of sharp effects. The newer umbrella concept was cleaner, but flatter.

### Finding 2: the current strongest route is scene realism, not concept inflation

The paper works best when it treats advisor group chats as a tiny but emotionally exact institutional scene. `Call-out effect`, `first-responder discount`, and `advisor red-envelope effect` land harder than a large abstract master concept.

### Finding 3: the folk taxonomy is worth keeping, but only as role language

`gatekeeping dog`, `muddy-water fish`, `dragging bottle`, `first-author junior`, and the procurement-reimbursement bottleneck are funny because they are recognizable. They should stay vivid, but they should not multiply into twenty new theory labels.

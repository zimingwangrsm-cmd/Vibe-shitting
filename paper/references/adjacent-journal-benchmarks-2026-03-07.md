# Adjacent Journal Benchmarks / 相近期刊对照笔记

This note records the closest public comparison set used to sharpen the manuscript's editorial style.

## Official comparison set

### 1. Rubbish

- About: https://rubbish-journal.org/zh/about
- Example article: https://rubbish-journal.org/zh/articles/578ebf58-f8e5-48cf-94c1-7405c85f11c6
- Core lesson:
  - use a real-journal shell
  - elevate low-status topics with very serious framing
  - let the emptiness of the object become the joke

### 2. The Journal of Immaterial Science

- Home: https://www.immaterialscience.org/
- Core lesson:
  - small absurd premises can support full academic treatment
  - titles and references can carry humor without breaking the formal voice

### 3. JABDE

- About: https://jabde.com/about/
- Core lesson:
  - parody works best when the article is fully committed to method language
  - humor can live in hypothesis framing, study design, and variable naming

### 4. Annals of Improbable Research

- What it is: https://improbable.com/whatis/
- Core lesson:
  - make people laugh, then think
  - improbable research is strongest when it reveals something real about institutions or habits

### 5. Speculative Grammarian

- About: https://specgram.com/about.html
- Core lesson:
  - deadpan consistency beats isolated punchlines
  - sustained pseudo-technical voice can be funnier than explicit jokes

## What to absorb into this manuscript

- Keep the article straight-faced.
- Put the strongest joke inside the core result, not only the title.
- Let role labels feel like institutional positions, not random memes.
- Let the tables and captions do part of the comedy.
- Prefer one memorable editorial hook over many medium-strength concepts.

## What not to copy

- Do not pivot away from SHIT's core taste into pure absurdism.
- Do not make the article depend on obscure insider references.
- Do not replace the current small-scene realism with a sprawling parody universe.

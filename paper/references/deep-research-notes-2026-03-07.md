## Purpose / 用途

## Theory Stack / 理论栈

### Signaling and Reputation

- Spence (1973): observable signals communicate hidden quality under asymmetric information.
- Kreps & Wilson (1982): repeated interaction makes small visible actions reputationally durable.

### Chronemics and CMC

- Walther (1992, 1996): users infer social meaning from mediated interaction even when face-to-face cues are reduced.
- Walther & Tidwell (1995): chronemics can function as relational cueing in computer-mediated communication.
- Tidwell & Walther (2002): impression formation can be built gradually through lean media.
- Kalman et al. (2013): online chronemics convey social information.
- Ziano & Wang (2021): response delay changes how email senders are perceived.

### Visibility, Status, and Multi-Person Coordination

- Leonardi (2014): communication visibility turns interaction traces into public organizational signals.
- Berger, Cohen, & Zelditch (1972): status structures shape participation expectations.
- Darley & Latané (1968): diffusion of responsibility in multi-person settings.
- Diekmann (1985, 1993): volunteer's dilemma and asymmetric volunteering logic.

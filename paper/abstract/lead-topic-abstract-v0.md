# Abstract v0 / 摘要 v0

## English

This paper proposes a signaling-game model for reply latency in advisor group chats.  
We argue that graduate students optimize not for speed alone, but for a narrow band of delay that maximizes perceived diligence while minimizing the risk of additional task assignment. Using a mixed-method design composed of message annotation, latency bucketing, and payoff-matrix reconstruction, we identify a stable strategy cluster: replies that arrive late enough to imply ongoing work, yet early enough to preserve reliability. We further show that message type, hierarchy depth, and emoji density interact with delay tolerance. The findings suggest that response latency in academic chat groups functions less as communication behavior and more as a reputational instrument.

## 中文

本文提出一个用于解释导师群聊响应延迟的信号博弈模型。  
我们认为，研究生优化的并不是单纯的“回复速度”，而是一段狭窄的延迟区间：它既要让自己看起来足够勤奋，又要尽量降低被继续分配任务的风险。通过消息标注、延迟分桶与收益矩阵重建，我们识别出一个相对稳定的策略簇：回复需要晚到足以暗示“我一直在忙”，又要早到足以维持“我仍然可靠”的印象。研究还表明，消息类型、层级结构和表情密度会共同影响可接受的回复时延。由此可见，学术群聊中的响应延迟，与其说是一种沟通行为，不如说是一种声誉工具。

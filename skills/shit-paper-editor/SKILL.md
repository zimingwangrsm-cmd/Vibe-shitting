# SHIT Paper Editor

Use this skill to convert a loose joke manuscript into a disciplined SHIT-style paper.

## Workflow

1. Open the current canonical manuscripts first.
   In this repo, start with `paper/final/read-seen-ignored_submission_en.md` and `paper/final/read-seen-ignored_submission_zh.md`.

2. Lock the concept stack before rewriting prose.
   Keep `1` main bias or proposition, at most `3` main mechanisms, and at most `2` moderators. Demote extra funny labels into examples, tables, or glossary notes instead of headline theory.

3. Standardize first mention of every named concept.
   When a term first appears, write these four moves in order:
   - state that the paper proposes the term
   - define the term in one precise sentence
   - explain how the term is operationalized or validated
   - anchor it to theory or references

4. Keep the SHIT tone straight-faced.
   Prefer over-precise institutional truth to random absurdity. The humor should come from formalizing a tiny but painful social fact until it becomes ridiculous.

5. Treat comic roles as vernacular institutions.
   Terms like `first-author junior`, `procurement steward`, or `muddy-water fish` should clarify role inequality, not explode into uncontrolled side jokes.

6. Tighten the paper's spine before adding new jokes.
   Unify:
   - the main problem
   - the main assumptions
   - the main hypotheses
   - the main findings
   - the outrageous but defensible conclusions

7. Polish the packaging layer.
   If the manuscript title, author line, affiliation, keyword set, or core concept changes, sync the paired EN/ZH file, the glossary, and the review note in the same pass.

8. Finish with an editorial review.
   Re-read the latest manuscript as if you were screening for SHIT. Findings should focus on novelty, humor density, academic camouflage, concept discipline, and shareability.

## Constraints

- Keep the paper academically legible even when the conclusions become deranged.
- Avoid naming more things unless the new term clearly earns its place.
- Keep Chinese and English aligned conceptually, not word-for-word mechanically.
- Prefer system satire over attacks on real people.

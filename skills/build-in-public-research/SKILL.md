# Build In Public Research

Use this skill to turn working research into durable public artifacts instead of isolated edits.

## Workflow

1. Advance the substantive artifact first.
   Finish the paper, figure, script, or review change before writing the public recap.

2. Preserve the trail.
   Update the relevant phase folder or social draft so the repo keeps a visible record of why the change happened and what it produced.

3. Append the persistent memo.
   In this workspace, append a short turn summary to `../CODEX_MEMO.md` after each material work cycle.

4. Convert the change into one GitHub-facing and one social-facing asset.
   Typical targets in this repo are:
   - `social/github/*.md`
   - `social/xiaohongshu/*.md`

5. Keep the project storyline stable.
   Readers should be able to tell:
   - what changed
   - why it matters
   - what the current best artifact is
   - what comes next

6. Update the repo's public landing surface when the phase meaning changes.
   Usually this means `README.md`, and sometimes the latest cover or current-phase links.

## Constraints

- Do not publish progress without leaving a repo trace.
- Do not create social copy that points at stale files.
- Keep the public narrative consistent with the actual artifact state.
